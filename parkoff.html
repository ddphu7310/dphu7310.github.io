Introduction

With all our wireframes completed, we transferred them into the InVision prototyping tool for convenience when evaluating our concept, as they can display prototypes on mobile which correctly simulates the nature of our concept. With this iteration, we want to test the fluidity and aesthetic quality of the app. This will mean that we need to evaluate the user’s interactions with every aspect of each screen. We had a total of 3 participants for the evaluation phase where all 3 were classmates.
Due to time constrictions, we used a very familiar evaluation method, we used think-aloud user testing as we believe we can get the most information about our prototype with this method in a short amount of time. Evidently, all 3 user testing sessions lasted between 5 and 10 minutes.
We chose this Think-Aloud user testing Method as it helped us understand participants’ real-time thoughts and emotions as they occur, and as they attempt to work through issues they encounter. We decided to conduct 3 tests to gather as much data as possible.

InVision Prototype

Evaluation Results

Scenario 1
You have a dentist appointment and your car had recently dropped you off at the clinic. You will need to find parking for your car as would need at least 3 hours since after the appointment you have to run a few errands.

Task
Select a parking spot in your area for at least 3 hours.

  • 2 participants were successful but 1 participant needed assistance to complete the task.

Takeaways/Recommendations
Participants suggested that the car in the “currently parking” screen should be animated as if the car is automatically driving towards the parking spot, this would be a nice novelty way to visually indicate that the car is parking until the time limit card pops up. Participants also commented on the on the nature of going back to to view previous options.

Scenario 2
Your appointment went a bit too long and you will need more than 2 hours for your errands meaning that you will go over your parking spot.

Task
Request more time or repark the car.

  • All 3 participants were successful in completing the task.

Takeaways/Recommendations
Since all participants were successful they didn't have much hesitation in proceeding with this step.

Scenario 3
*restart prototype* You have a new credit card and would like to add the card into the ParkOff database and use it as the primary payment option when paying for parking.

Task
Add a new payment method into ParkOff.

  • 1 participant was successful but the other 2 participants needed assistance to complete the task.

Takeaways/Recommendations
Participants suggested the way to look through cars and cards by swiping left/right on the pictures. The settings should be able to be on every screen and have the settings icon be easily accessible.

Next Steps

Although the evaluation was conducted under undesirable time conditions, we were still able to innovate new aspects into our eventual final concept and prototype. Participants were extremely fond with the aesthetical properties of the InVision prototype as their feedback were, for the majority focused on improving and adding new transitional cues and animating functions within the app.
